üîç What is the CFAA?

The Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA) is a United States cybersecurity law enacted in 1986 to combat computer-related crimes.

Key aspects of the CFAA:

  • Prohibits unauthorized computer access
  • Covers various computer-related crimes
  • Applies to federal and certain private computers
  • Provides criminal and civil penalties

The CFAA imposes severe penalties for violations:

  • Fines
  • Imprisonment (up to 10 years for first offenses)
  • Property forfeiture

Penalties vary based on the violation:

  • Accessing to obtain information: Up to 1-5 years
  • Accessing a government computer: Up to 1-10 years
  • Damaging a computer: Up to 10-20 years
  • Trafficking in passwords: Up to 1-10 years
